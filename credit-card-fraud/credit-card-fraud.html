You receive a call from your brother residing in USA asking the CVV and expiry date of your credit card. Should you reveal these details?

NEVER reveal confidential details like credit card number, expiry date, CVV, PIN and OTP over call even to a trusted person. If cardholders reveal their PIN, they could be liable for any fraudulent transactions on the account.

You have received your new credit card and are required to set a pin. Is your birthdate a good option for the same?

×

Never use apparent information as your PIN, such as your birthdate or home address. These details can be collected from social media and other publicly available places by fraudsters. Unless your Facebook page is set to private, strangers could use your birthday wishes and memories to gain access to your credit card account.

If your card if safe and secure with you, does that guarantee the fact that a fraudster cannot use it to perform frauds?

×

Scammers can make purchases with your credit card even if the real card isn't there. Many of the secure payment protections built into the card are bypassed when a cardholder just enters their credit card number, whether over the phone or online. Fraudsters can get this information from abandoned bills or receipts, or by overhearing your credit card information being recited aloud. In many of these incidents, both the cardholder and the merchant are defrauded.

Rohan has lost his credit card and is unable to find it. What should he do next?

×

Always report a lost or stolen credit or debit card as soon as possible. Your credit card issuer may be able to place a hold on your account until a replacement card arrives in the mail. Many banking institutions also provide cardholders with the ability to lock and unlock their cards via an app, ensuring that they are not victims of payment fraud.

You may receive calls from your credit card company asking for personal information such as your address and email.

×

Credit card frauds can spoof your bank or include real-time fraud alerts, which inform cardholders through text, email, or automated phone call whenever a purchase is made to validate the transaction. Your bank, on the other hand, will never urge you to click a link or supply personal information via these channels. Always use the secure banking web or app, or contact the phone number listed on the back of your card.
